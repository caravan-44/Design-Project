Largemouth Bass


Largemouth are the most fished type of bass in North America. They like to live in calm, warm shallow water of about 3 to 9 feet. They also like to be concealed by vegetation, and murky water. To tell them apart from their closeley related relative the Smallmouth Bass, you can look at their mouthes, and if it extends from their upper lip, it's a Largemouth. They also have a dark horizontal line often broken on their body as another way to tell them apart. They also behave differently, and like to lurk under a log or rock before lunging for their prey.



Smallmouth Bass


Smallmouth bass are the second most fished bass in North America. They like to hang around in cool, clear and deeper water than largemouth, which is also a good way to tell them apart. Smallmouth do not have the overhung lowerlip, which gives them a "smaller mouth". These are also probably the hardest bass to fish for, known for their agressive behavior and fighting to get off the bait. They have bronze to olive coloring in dark bars.

There is one more species, the striped bass, but it cannot be targeted and i've never fished it, so i'm not including it.
