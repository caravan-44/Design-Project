Styles of fishing





Spin Fishing (spinning rod)

Spin fishing is one of the easiest and most versatile techniques to fish bass. Fishing with a spinning rod is easy for large mouth and smallmouth. The bait with the biggest success rate is most likely the soft plastic baits, like Gary Yamamoto and Dark Sleeper. You can also succeed with baits like small spinner baits, and small plastic lures. This is one of the best rods for beginners.





Bait Caster

Baitcasters are not as beginner friendly, but they are more versatile than the spinning rod. They are favored because they can handle heavy weights better, and the accuracy and hookset rivals over other rods. They are especially good for jigs, big spinner baits, crankbaits and getting through weeds and dock fishing. It does have a bigger learning curve, and it is harder to get on the first try due to the difficult reel mechanism.





Trolling

While trolling isn't as comonly used for bass, more centered around walleye or trout, it can still work, if you are targeting one area, or there are lots in deep water. Trolling uses a rod that is more alike with the spinning rod, but it has a depth counter on the reel. Trolling is basically where you move the boat with the lure in deep water. You will mainly use spin baits, crankbaits and swimmer rigs. Trolling is more for finding bass in a big area, as it covers lots of area.
